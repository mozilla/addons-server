Improved Privacy Settings

Mozilla wants to know if we can improve upon and expand our privacy features for Firefox Users. Participating in this study will help us find out!

What will happen next

If you agree to participate:

  1. We will add additional functionality to improve your privacy with Firefox.
  2. You will see a new browser icon that will allow you to report broken websites to Mozilla.
  3. Continue using Firefox as you normally would..
  4. When you see a broken site, use the browser icon to report it.
  5. After 28 days, the study will end on its own. Your Firefox will go back to normal.
  6. We will ask you to fill out a quick optional survey to tell us about your experience. The survey should take less than 5 minutes to complete.

Leaving the study

The study will expire on its own in 28 days. You may leave the study early. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. Open the browser icon to report broken websites.
  2. Click "Disable Privacy Study"
  3. If you opt out of the study early we will ask you to fill out a survey. We’re interested in hearing about your experience, even if you didn’t participate in the entire study.

Opting out of a study does not prevent you from participating in future studies.

Your privacy

All Shield Studies

Every Shield Study collects data about important study events, such as install, uninstall, daily usage, and end of study. Studies also include an optional survey.

This data is associated with Firefox Telemetry collection.

This Particular Study

In addition to the data collected by all Shield Studies and your voluntary answers to the followup survey, here are the key things you should know about what is happening when you participate in this study:

  • When you choose to report a page as broken, we record the high-level domain name (e.g., mozilla.org) of the page that you report as broken.
  • We also ask for additional information regarding how the reported webpage broke, other attributes of the page, and any additional comments.
  • No personal information or data will be collected or recorded during the study, and we only receive information about sites you choose to report as broken.

You can learn more about the data collection for this study on our metrics document.

You help make Firefox better

At Mozilla, we pride ourselves on building products for you, the user! That’s why we need your help. You help us by letting us observe how you use new features and by telling us your opinions about them.

By participating in this study, you will help us make better decisions on your behalf and directly shape the future of Firefox.
