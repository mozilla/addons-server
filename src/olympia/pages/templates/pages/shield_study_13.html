Understanding the Web

Mozilla wants to know more about your knowledge and opinions of the Internet while you browse. To do so, we have partnered with the Annenberg School for Communication Research at the University of Pennsylvania to conduct a field study on this subject.

What will happen next

If you agree to participate:

  1. We will add additional features to Firefox that provide informational content about the websites you visit.
  2. Continue using Firefox as you normally would. You may not notice any changes immediately.
  3. After 28 days, the study will end on its own. Your Firefox will go back to normal.
  4. During the study, we will ask you to fill out a few quick optional surveys to tell us about your experience. The surveys should take less than 5 minutes to complete.

Leaving the study

The study will expire on its own in 28 days. You may leave the study early. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. Type about:addons into the location bar, and press enter.
  2. Find Web Health Study in the extensions list.
  3. Click the remove button.
  4. If you opt out of the study early we will ask you to fill out a survey. We’re interested in hearing about your experience, even if you didn’t participate in the entire study.

Opting out of a study does not prevent you from participating in future studies.

Your privacy

All Shield Studies

Every Shield Study collects data about important study events, such as install, uninstall, daily usage, and end of study. Studies also include an optional survey.

This data is associated with Firefox Telemetry collection and treated in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

This Particular Study

In addition to the data collected by all Shield Studies, here are the key things you should know about what is happening when you participate in this study:

  • We will record the number of times you visit certain pre-defined set of websites (only top-level hostnames will be collected, e.g. www.amazon.com)
  • We will record information about your interactions with notifications coming from this study
  • The study will not be active, and no information will be recorded, while using Private Browsing Mode
  • We will not collect any identifying information such as names, phone numbers, or email addresses
  • This study is covered under IRB #827604 from the University of Pennsylvania

You can learn more about the data collection for this study, including the pre-defined list of websites in our Wiki.
