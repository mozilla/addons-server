Page Reload Study

Mozilla wants to figure out the best way to detect when you are having trouble viewing a website. This will allow us to figure out why the website might be broken. Participating in this study will help us find out!

What will happen next

If you agree to participate:

  1. Continue using Firefox as you normally would. Depending on how you use the Web, you may not notice any changes.
  2. You might occasionally receive prompts that ask you about why you reload (refresh) a web page in Firefox.
  3. After a few weeks, the study will end on its own. Your Firefox will go back to normal.
  4. We will ask you to fill out a quick optional survey to tell us about your experience. The survey should take less than 5 minutes to complete.

Leaving the study

The study will expire on its own in a few weeks. You may leave the study early. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. Type about:addons into the location bar, and press enter.
  2. Find Page Reload Study 1 in the Extensions list.
  3. Click the remove button.
  4. If you opt out of the study early we will ask you to fill out a survey. We’re interested in hearing about your experience, even if you didn’t participate in the entire study.

Opting out of a study does not prevent you from participating in future studies.

Your privacy

All Shield Studies

Every Shield Study collects data about important study events, such as install, uninstall, daily usage, and end of study. Studies also include an optional survey.

This data is associated with Firefox Telemetry collection.

This particular study

In addition to the data collected by all Shield Studies, Mozilla will collect information about:

  • High level domains and subdomains of websites you visit that may be broken
  • Your interaction associated with reloading websites

Mozilla will keep this information private; any data will only be shared in aggregate or anonymous form.

You can learn more about the data collection for this study on our Data Collection wiki page.
